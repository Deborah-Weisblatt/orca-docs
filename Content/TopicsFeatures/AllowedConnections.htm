Allowed Connections

Allowed connections are the rules governing network traffic in your cluster, constituting a policy that can be enforced on your cluster whenever you choose. These rules can define namespaces, services and DNS names. Unlike firewall rules, they do not define IP addresses. Initially the list is empty and new allowed connections are defined manually, optionally assisted by the discovery engine.

Your policy will always be in one of two modes - learning mode or enforcement mode. When in learning mode, will not apply any limitations on traffic in the cluster. When in enforce mode, will apply limitations on the traffic. Switching modes is done in Settings. To see how to get here look here.

Actions Available on this Screen

  • Add an allowed connection by entering details yourself
  • Add an allowed connection using details discovered by
  • Delete an allowed connection
  • Lock / unlock an allowed connection
  • Reset allowed connections
  • Apply on firewall

Adding an Allowed Connection

You can specify an allowed connection from a namespace in your cluster to a domain outside of the cluster.

  1. Click on the Add icon ( or depending on the currently selected policy mode). The Add Allowed Connection window is displayed.

  2. Enter the details:

    Fields on the Screen

    Field Name

    Value
    From Click in the field to display the list of available namespaces and then click on the required value
    To

    Type a valid domain name (DNS). Optionally use the wildcard character on the left of the name. This will allow multiple protocols and/or sub-domains to be included in the same rule.

    Examples: api.example.io, *.example.com
  3. Click Add Rule to save. The rule will be added to the list.

Deleting an Allowed Connection

Rules can be deleted from the security policy at any time.

  1. Hover over the line for the appropriate rule and click when it appears on the line.
  2. The allowed connection will be removed.

Locking / Unlocking an Allowed Connection

The purpose of locking rules is to prevent them from being deleted when resetting allowed connections. However, a locked rule can individually be deleted in the same way as an unlocked rule. Rules can be locked and unlocked in the security policy at any time.

To lock a rule:

  1. Hover over the line for the appropriate rule and click the lock icon when it appears on the line.
  2. Thereafter, the rule will display the lock icon all the time.

Resetting Allowed Connections

Resetting allowed connections restores your policy to a base-line of allowed rules. All rules that have been locked constitute your base-line and if no rules have been locked, your base-line is a policy with no rules whatsoever.

  1. Click on Actions to display the Actions menu.

  2. Click on Reset Allowed Connections from the menu.

  3. All allowed connections will be deleted from the security policy, except those that are locked.

Apply on Firewall

The apply on firewalls function is available only if you have a network policy change management solution such as integrated with . It applies the security policy you have defined in to the devices managed by submitting one or more firewall change tickets to the network policy change management solution. A ticket will be submitted for each namespace in your policy.

After the apply on firewalls process is initiated, it cannot be initiated again until all tickets have been implemented. If not all tickets have been implemented following a prior attempt, the menu option will be greyed out and followed by "(in-progress)". This status will remain until all prior tickets have been resolved and implemented or deleted in the network policy change management system.

After the apply on firewalls process is initiated, the tickets created can be seen on the Firewall Tickets Screen, together with their status.

  1. Click on Actions to display the Actions Menu.

  2. Click on Apply on Firewalls from the menu. This will initiate the process and change the option in the Actions Menu to be greyed out and followed by "(in-progress)". You cannot initiate the process further time until the menu option is restored to its normal state.

How to Get Here

From the main menu select Policy then Allowed Connections
