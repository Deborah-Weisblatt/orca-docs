Allowed Connections

Allowed connections are the rules governing network traffic in your cluster, constituting a policy that can be enforced on your cluster whenever you choose. These rules can define namespaces, services and DNS names. Unlike firewall rules, they do not define IP addresses. Initially the list is empty; allowed connections are defined manually, assisted by the discovery engine.

Your policy will always be in one of two modes - learning mode or enforcement mode. When in learning mode, will not apply any limitations on traffic in the cluster. When in enforce mode, will apply limitations on the traffic. Switching modes is done in Settings

Actions Available on this Screen

  • Add an allowed connection
  • Delete an allowed connection
  • Lock an allowed connection
  • Reset allowed connections
  • Apply on firewall

Adding an Allowed Connection

Click on the Add icon ( or depending on currently selected policy mode). The Add Allowed Connection window is displayed

Click on the empty namespace field under 'From' and select the appropriate namespace from the list from which to create the rule.

Click on the empty Domain Name field under 'to'. You can use a wildcard prefix * (e.g. *.example.com)

Click Add Rule.to save. The line will be added to the list.

Deleting Allowed Connection

  • Hover over the line for the desired connection and click when it appears at the end of the line.

  • The line will be removed.

Resetting Allowed Connections

  • Click on Actions at the top of the screen to display the actions menu.

  • Click on Reset Allowed Connections from the actions menu.

  • All allowed connections will be removed.

How do I get here?

From the menu select Policy then View allowed connections
