Service Graph

The Service Graph graphically displays all the active traffic between the services used in your web application, and external services outside the cluster. The specific time frame that traffic is treated as active is configurable. If traffic between two services has not occurred during the time frame selected, the services will not appear in the Services Graph. Every service displayed has an associated color-coded that shows how secure the service is.

Actions Available on this Screen

  • View Service Details - Click on a service to view details about a specific service.

  • View details - Click on the of a service for expanded details about known vulnerabilities for that service.
  • View reputation information for external end-points

  • Configure traffic time frame - Select the desired to traffic time frame.

  • Refresh - Click to for a refresh of the Service Graph.

  • General actions

Security Score Details

View Reputation Information

Clicking on an external end-point will display an analysis of the site's reputation including information about the domain and a high-level vulnerability summary.

The analysis information is provided through a 3rd party called Reposify. If Reposify doesn't have data for the site, you will be automatically redirected to the Talos reputation website (www.talosintelligence.com).

Traffic Time Frame

The Traffic Time Frame is the length of time that considers a connection as active. Connections are displayed in the Service Graph for services that have had active traffic during the time frame selected. If no traffic has originated from or into a service during the time frame selected, that service will not appear in the Service Graph.

How to Get Here

From the menu in the left panel, click Service Graph.
