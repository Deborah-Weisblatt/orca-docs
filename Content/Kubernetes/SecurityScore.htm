Image Scanning and

The is an assessment of code vulnerability based on the scanning of a Docker image. It is presented as a value from A+, the least vulnerable to F, the most vulnerable. A score of A+ indicates no vulnerabilities were detected whatsoever. The appears in a number of different places in including the Service Graph and Pods Screen and additionally can be displayed in your code repository in the form of a badge.

An image scan is initiated:

  1. Every time a container is deployed to your cluster. detects this event automatically.
  2. As part of your CI/CD pipeline. This requires the addition of commands to the CI/CD script.

To perform the analysis, pulls the container images from their registry and the connection from the to the registry is just like any other new connection; it will show up in New Connections and you will need to add it to your policy to allow future pulls to work. If you have a firewall, you must allow every cluster node access to your container image registries.

The displayed is always for the last image scanned, regardless of what triggered the scan. Badges also refer to the last image scanned unless the image tag is included in the path.

Calculation

  1. assigns a weighted value according to the severity of each vulnerability identified. The severities from highest to lowest are:

    Severity Weighting
    Defcon1 100
    Critical 50
    High 11
    Medium 1.1
    Low 0.11
    Negligible 0.011

    Unknown

    0

  2. A numerical value is calculated between 1 and 100, based on the quantity and weighting of each severity, where 100 is the most secure and 1 is the least secure.

  3. The calculated numerical value is converted to the according to the table below.

    Numerical Values
    A+ 100
    A 90-99
    B 80-89
    C 70-79
    D 60-69
    E 50-59
    F 1-49
