What Next? Using

You've just installed on your cluster and you've seen there are services listed, so everything is working OK. Now it's time to get working for you. Start with the main tasks and when you're ready you can look at 's automation and integration capabilities.

Main Tasks

These are the more frequent tasks you'll be handling:

  • Add users to your project.
  • Review the cluster and its activity. To see traffic, make sure there has been activity in your cluster for an hour at least. Each of the following will give you visibility into different levels of the cluster and expose vulnerabilities: Dashboard, Service Graph, Services, Nodes, Pods, Services, Nodes, New Connections, Kubernetes Network Policies.
  • Build and maintain a security policy. Review connections discovered by that you haven't allowed in the policy, add allowed connections to the policy and fine tune the policy by adding / removing as required.
  • Enforce the policy on Kubernetes. See Export as Network Policies.

Automation and Integration

After getting to grips with the primary tasks, check out 's advanced capabilities:

  • Set up immutable policy control - security policy creation built into your CI/CD pipeline.
  • Scanning of Docker container images for vulnerabilities.
  • Apply your policy on firewalls via integration with .
  • Apply your policy to Microsoft's Azure Firewall.
  • Put a badge on your repo to display the security score of your container.
