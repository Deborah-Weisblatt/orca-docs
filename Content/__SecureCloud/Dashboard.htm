Dashboard

The dashboard is a snapshot of your compliance with security policy and best practices in your cloud account settings and k8s clusters. It is made up of widgets representing common risks, each showing a compliance score that indicates your robustness with respect to the risk.

  • Filter the display - click All, or Kubernetes
  • Drill down to see risk detail of the risk - click on the desired

Risk Detail

Click on a to drill down to the relevant detail.

Risky Ports

The Risky Ports shows the compliance score of your public cloud environment with respect to high-risk ports - the fewer high-risk ports you have, the higher the compliance score. Click on the to display all applications that allow access through these ports.

What are risky ports?

Recommended best practice:

  • In your cloud account security definitions, do not allow ingress from any of the ports listed above.

Permissive Access

The Permissive access shows the compliance score of your public cloud environment with respect to overly permissive ingress access. The fewer instances of permissive access you have, the higher the compliance score. Click on the to display all applications with permissive access.

What is permissive access?

Recommended best practice:

  • In your cloud account security definitions, avoid the use of unlimited ranges; allow traffic to assets only through well-defined ports and IP addresses.

Policy Violations

The policy violations shows the compliance score of your public cloud environment with respect to your security policy. The closer your cloud vendor security definitions match your policy, the higher the compliance score. Click on the to display all applications with policy violations.

What is a policy violation?

Recommended best practice: 

  • Review your applications and assets and resolve all violations.

Tag Usage

The Tag Usage shows the compliance score of your public cloud environment with respect to usage of tags on your assets. The fewer assets you have without tags, the higher the compliance score. Click on the to display all assets that have no tags.

What is meant by Tags

Recommended best practice:

  • Use tags on all assets to define important asset characteristics such as application and environment, as described in Global Application Visibility.

Kubernetes Vulnerabilities

The Kubernetes Vulnerabilities shows the compliance score of your Kubernetes clusters with respect to known vulnerabilities in your Kubernetes environment - the fewer vulnerabilities you have, the higher the compliance score. Click on the to display all clusters containing known Kubernetes vulnerabilities.

What are Kubernetes Vulnerabilities?

Recommended best practice:

  • Regularly review the Kubernetes vulnerabilities in your environment and make sure you have the latest patches in place.

Privileged Workloads
