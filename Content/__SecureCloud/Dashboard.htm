Dashboard

The dashboard is a snapshot of your compliance with security policy and best practices in your cloud account settings and k8s clusters. It is made up of widgets representing common risks, each showing a compliance score indicating your robustness against the risk.

  • Filter the display - click All, or Kubernetes
  • Drill down to see detail of the risk - click on the desired

Detail

Click on a to drill down to the relevant detail.

Risky Ports

The Risky Ports shows the compliance score of your public cloud environment with respect to high-risk ports - the fewer high-risk ports you have, the higher the compliance score.

Port 23, 110, 135-139, 445 and 3389 are considered high risk for various different reasons and allowing traffic to reach your assets from any of these could leave it open to attack. Click on the to display all applications that allow access through these ports.

Recommended best practice: In your cloud account security definitions, do not allow ingress from any of the ports listed above.

Permissive Access

The Permissive access shows the compliance score of your public cloud environment with respect to ingress from an unlimited sources. The fewer instances of permissive access you have, the higher the compliance score. Click on the to display all applications that have permissive access.

Permissive access is where UDP or TCP ingress is allowed in any of the following circumstances:

  • From any port (Min=0, Max=65535)
  • Via transport access from an unlimited range of IPs (class A subnet mask e.g. 255.0.0.0/8)
  • From any source (Source=ANY)

Recommended best practice: In your cloud account security definitions, avoid the use of unlimited ranges; allow traffic to assets only through well-defined ports and IP addresses.

Policy Violations

The policy violations shows the compliance score of your public cloud environment with respect to your security policy. The closer your cloud vendor security definitions match your policy, the higher the compliance score. Click on the to display all applications with policy violations.

Recommended best practice: Review your applications and assets and resolve all violations.

Internet Access

The internet access shows the compliance score of your public cloud environment with respect to access to or from the internet. The fewer the connectivity possibilities with the internet, the higher the compliance score. Click on the to display all applications that have access to the internet.

Recommended best practice: Limit internet access to as few devices as possible and ensure only approved assets have such access.

Tag Usage

The Kubernetes Vulnerabilities shows the compliance score of your Kubernetes clusters with respect to usage of tags on your assets. The fewer assets you have without tags, the higher the compliance score.

 

The tag usage shows the compliance of your assets

 

based on the premise that all of your assets should have tags for your security policy to be sound.

presents a comparison between applications or assets that have asset tags associated with them and those that do not. Click on the to display all assets that have no tags.

Recommended best practice: Use tags to define important asset characteristics such as application and environment, as described in Global Application Visibility.

The dashboard gives you a snapshot of your Kubernetes security issues vulnerabilities through charts:

Network Policy Namespace Coverage

This chart compares the number of namespaces having at least one Kubernetes network policy with namespaces that have no network policies. The number of k8s network policies is not reflected here.

Namespace Vulnerabilities

This chart shows the number of namespaces for which container vulnerabilities were found against the highest severity of vulnerability found. The number of vulnerabilities found in each namespace is not reflected here. The vulnerability level for a namespace is the largest vulnerability found in any of its containers.

Privileged Workloads

The Privileged Workload shows the compliance score of your Kubernetes clusters with respect to privileged workloads - the fewer privileged workloads you have, the higher the compliance score. Click on the to display all clusters containing privileged workloads.

A privileged workload can be a pod with a permissive pod security policy or a container that has its privileged parameter set to true.

Kubernetes Vulnerabilities

The Kubernetes Vulnerabilities shows the compliance score of your Kubernetes clusters with respect to known vulnerabilities in your Kubernetes environment- the fewer vulnerabilities you have, the higher the compliance score. Click on the to display all clusters containing known Kubernetes vulnerabilities.
