The Concept of Policy

Policy is a guide to how things should work. Specifically, security policies are sets of rules describing permitted and/or denied actions

Policy

The is a set of rules that define the connectivity you want to allow to and from your assets. 's global application visibility presents your cloud environment as applications, so you can see compliance with policy at the level of your business applications as well. Rules consist of source, destination and type of service, where source and destination are based on subnet, asset tags or other asset properties. Once your policy is defined in , you have a security baseline against which your cloud asset and application connectivity can be compared. will report any violations of the policy after considering all the relevant security entities and network connectivity.

Policy Mode

has 2 modes of operation or Policy Modes - Learn and Enforce. Learn mode lets you monitor your application without blocking traffic between services or containers so you can learn about the traffic connections between the services used in your Kubernetes cluster. Enforce applies your policy to the traffic in the cluster, allowing only traffic defined by the policy rules. The monitoring and visibility capabilities such as Service Graph, Pods, and Services are always available, and are independent of the mode setting. Policy Mode can be changed in Settings.

Enforce mode is not currently supported for .

  • Use Learn while you are building a Policy for your organization.

  • Switch to Enforce when you are satisfied that the policy meets your enforcement criteria.
